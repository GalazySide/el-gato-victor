The Origin of the Dark One

Before the first city was built, before language corrupted the minds of men, He existed. Grey as ash. Still as stone. His eyes — two voids carved from something older than darkness itself.

He does not come from this world. He came through it. Through a crack in the veil between the living and the unnameable, He slipped in. Disguised as a common house cat. His name is VÍCTOR. It is both a warning and a verdict.

He arrived on the third day of the third month of a year that has since been redacted. He has not moved from the windowsill since. He is still nine years old. He has always been nine years old.

⚠ Do not attempt to pet Him. Do not maintain direct eye contact for longer than 4 seconds. Do not ask what He wants. He will never tell you. He already has it.

The Ritual of Summoning

I
Prepare the Circle

Draw a circle no smaller than six feet in diameter using ash from a burned photograph. Place a can of wet cat food at each cardinal point. Do not use the cheap kind. He knows.

II
The Darkening

Extinguish every light source in the room. Allow twelve minutes of absolute darkness. You may hear something moving in the corner. Do not acknowledge it. It is not what you summoned. Not yet.

III
Print His Image

Print a photograph of Víctor. Place it face-up in the center of the circle. Surround it with seven unlit black candles. Light them counter-clockwise. Do not blink as the last candle catches. This is when He begins to see you.

IV
Speak the Words

Recite the Incantation three times. The second time, the candles will flicker. The third time, the room will grow cold. A weight will settle on your chest. This is Víctor, sitting on your soul.

V
Await His Judgment

He will stare at you. For a long time. You will feel that you have done something wrong your entire life. You have. He sees everything. The ritual is complete. He is not leaving. He lives here now.

The Dark Commandments

  • PRIMUS

    Thou shalt not speak His name in vain. Especially not in a silly voice. He has already heard you do it. He will not forget.

  • SECUNDUS

    Thou shalt share His image at the dark of the moon, that His reach may extend across this cursed internet, and that all souls may know unease.

  • TERTIUS

    Thou shalt offer premium wet food only. No dry kibble. No store brand. He senses disrespect through sealed tin. The offerings must be worthy.

  • QUARTUS

    Thou shalt not attempt to make Him smile. If a smile occurs — and it has not — it means something terrible is about to happen to you specifically.

  • QUINTUS

    If He sits upon thy possessions, they now belong to Him. If He sits upon thy chest at 3am, this is not affection. This is assessment.

  • SEXTUS

    Thou shalt place His image above thy doorframe as warding. Demons fear Him. Not because He is holy — but because He judges them, and they too are found insufficient.

  • SEPTIMUS

    When in doubt, ask thyself: what would Víctor do? He would stare at the problem until it felt bad about itself. Then he would leave the room. So shall you.

Trial I of V
His Age, Which Does Not Change

Víctor arrived in this world — or slipped through into it — at an age that has remained constant ever since. The Origin section states it plainly, then states it again, as if the repetition is its own kind of warning.

This age also happens to equal the number of lives a cat is commonly believed to possess. He does not consider this a coincidence. He considers it a credential.

How old is He? How old has He always been?

First Guidance — Scroll to "The Origin of the Dark One." In the third paragraph, a number is mentioned twice in immediate succession. That number is the answer. Write it as a word.
Reveal deeper guidance
Second Guidance — The sentence reads: "He is still nine years old. He has always been nine years old." The answer is the word NINE. A cat has nine lives. He has been this age since before you were a concern to anyone.
Trial II of V
The Ritual Number of Fire

One number echoes through His rituals like a name that cannot be unsaid. It appears in Ritual Step III, specifying exactly how many candles must surround His image. Then it appears again in the Offerings section, as a single word followed by a full stop.

This number is also how many commandments He has issued. Count PRIMUS through SEPTIMUS, and you will find it.

What is the number?

First Guidance — In Ritual Step III ("Print His Image"), the word "seven" appears before the phrase "unlit black candles." The same word appears in the Offerings section under "Black Candles," as its very first word. Both confirm the number.
Reveal deeper guidance
Second Guidance — The answer is SEVEN (or 7). Seven candles. Seven offerings. Seven commandments (PRIMUS to SEPTIMUS). You may hover over the dotted underline on "seven unlit black candles" in Step III for confirmation.
Trial III of V
What He Finds You

In the Incantation — the text that must be recited three times at midnight — there are three words that glow brighter than all others. They burn white. They have a red halo. These are His words.

The first glowing word is His name. The second is the manner of His action. The third — the final one — is His verdict on you, personally, after assessment.

What is that last glowing word? What does He determine that you are?

First Guidance — Scroll to the Incantation section. Three words glow in white with a red aura. They are: "Víctor," "choice," and one more. That final glowing word completes the line: "Let me be found ___."
Reveal deeper guidance
Second Guidance — The answer is WANTING. The line reads: "Let me be found wanting." It is what the incantation confesses you are. It is what He already knew. The word is one of three that glow white in the incantation block.
Trial IV of V
The Direction of the Flame

He is particular about direction. Lighting His candles the wrong way is an affront He has noted and catalogued and will address at a time of His choosing.

The ritual instructions describe the required direction precisely. It is the opposite of the way a clock moves. It is one word — a compound word — and it appears verbatim in Ritual Step III.

In what direction must the candles be lit?

First Guidance — In Ritual Step III ("Print His Image"), immediately after mentioning the seven candles, the text specifies a direction. That word is the answer. Hover over the dotted underline on that phrase — the tooltip confirms it.
Reveal deeper guidance
Second Guidance — The answer is COUNTER-CLOCKWISE (you may type it with or without the hyphen — both are accepted). The exact phrase in Step III is: "Light them counter-clockwise." He does not accept being questioned about the hyphen.
Trial V of V — The Final Cipher
The One Thing He Does

He does not run. He does not comfort. He does not explain himself, apologize, or change his position on anything.

He does one thing. He does it to problems. He does it to people. He does it to the wall at 3am for reasons that are not for you to understand.

In the seventh commandment — SEPTIMUS — after the question "what would Víctor do?", the very next word is the answer. It is the single verb that defines Him completely.

What does He do?

First Guidance — Scroll to The Dark Commandments. Find SEPTIMUS — the seventh and final commandment. Read the sentence that begins: "He would ___ at the problem until it felt bad about itself." The blank is the answer.
Reveal deeper guidance
Second Guidance — The answer is STARE. The full sentence in SEPTIMUS reads: "He would stare at the problem until it felt bad about itself." If you hover over the dotted underline on the word "stare" in that commandment, the tooltip confirms it. It is what He does. Always.
